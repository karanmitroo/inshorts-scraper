Britain's Andy Murray won the Shanghai Masters, his fifth title of 2016, defeating Spain's Roberto Bautista Agut 7-6(7-1), 6-1 on Sunday. World number two Murray won the China Open a week ago, besides winning the men's singles event at the Rio Olympics and Wimbledon earlier this year. He is now 915 points behind top-ranked Novak Djokovic in the ATP rankings.
