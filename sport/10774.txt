Delhi batsman Rishabh Pant became the world's youngest wicketkeeper to score a first-class triple century, making 308 aged 19 years 12 days, in the recently-concluded Maharashtra-Delhi match. He is now also the third youngest Indian to score a first-class triple century, behind Wasim Jaffer and Abhinav Mukund. Jaffer scored his first triple hundred in 1996, aged 18 years 265 days.
