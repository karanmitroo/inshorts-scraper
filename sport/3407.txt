The match between Shelby Rogers and Daria Gavrilova on Thursday lasted for three hours and 33 minutes, setting the record for the longest women's singles match in US Open history. Rogers defeated Gavrilova 7-6 (8-6), 4-6, 7-6 (7-5) in the second-round match. The previous record was three hours and 23 minutes, when Johanna Konta defeated Garbine Muguruza in 2015.
