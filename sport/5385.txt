Playing against India in the Independence Cup in Chennai on May 21, 1997, Pakistani top-order batsman Saeed Anwar scored 194 runs, the then highest individual score in ODI cricket. Anwar's 146-ball innings, which included 22 boundaries and five sixes, helped Pakistan post a total of 327/5. Notably, the next highest score in the innings was an unbeaten 39, by Inzamam-ul-Haq.
