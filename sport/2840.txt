New Zealand batsman Martin Guptill smashed Cricket World Cup's highest individual score of 237 against Windies in the quarter-final of the 2015 edition. Guptill's unbeaten 237 helped NZ register 393/6 while Windies were dismissed for 250, 13 runs more than that of Guptill. The right-handed batsman, who turned 31 on Saturday, hit 11 sixes and 24 fours during his knock.
