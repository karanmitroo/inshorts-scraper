 An Indian consumer awareness and complaints platform, Akosha, has raised about $16M (Rs 100 crore) in its third round of investment from Sequoia Capital, valuing the company at about $70-80M. The Delhi-based company has raised about $21.5 million so far. "We plan to raise another $50M in this year to scale up more," said Ankur Singla, co-founder of Akosha.
