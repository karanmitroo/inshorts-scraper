Baba Ramdev-founded Patanjali became India's second biggest consumer goods maker, reporting a revenue of ₹10,561 crore in the 2016-17 fiscal. The company earned the most from cow's ghee, which contributed ₹1,467 crore. Its toothpaste contributed ₹940 crore to the turnover, while Ayurvedic medicines contributed ₹870 crore. The company also became market leader in cow's ghee and Ayurvedic medicines.
