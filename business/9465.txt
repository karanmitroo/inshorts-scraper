The Jammu and Kashmir government has recorded an 11.75% growth year-on-year with excise tax revenue realisation of ₹512.20 crore in the first five months of the current fiscal. Despite more than two-and-a-half month-long unrest in the Kashmir Valley following the killing of Hizbul commander Burhan Wani, the collections rose ₹53.85 crore from the corresponding period a year ago.
