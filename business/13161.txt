Global equities witnessed their worst start to a year since 2000 with the MSCI All-Country World Index slumping 3.3% in 2016's first three trading days. Emerging markets fell to their lowest levels since 2009 after China let Yuan slide to a five-year low against the US Dollar. Meanwhile, the World Bank cut 2016's global growth forecasts by 0.4% to 2.9%
