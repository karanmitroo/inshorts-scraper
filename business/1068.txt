The Department of Telecommunications is likely to issue notices to five telecom operators to recover ₹2,578 crore following a CAG report. The CAG said the telcos, which include Tata Teleservices, Telenor, and Reliance Jio, understated revenues by over ₹14,800 crore causing shortfall to the government. The biggest defaulter among the five is Tata, with a total shortfall of ₹1,893.6 crore.
