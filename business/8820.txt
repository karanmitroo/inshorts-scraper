India's forex reserves fell from an all-time high to $367.64 billion, declining by $4.34 billion in the week ended October 7, following a slump in foreign currency assets (FCA), data showed. FCA, which constitutes the biggest share, dropped $4.31 billion to $342.39 billion. Meanwhile, gold reserves remained steady at $21.40 billion and SDR with the IMF declined by $10.4 million.
