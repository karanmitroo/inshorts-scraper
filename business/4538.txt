The prices of petrol and diesel were hiked by ₹1.23/litre and ₹0.89/litre respectively on Thursday. After the hike, Mumbai remains the most expensive city for fuel in India, with petrol costing ₹78.44/litre and diesel costing ₹61.67/litre. Bengaluru comes second with petrol at ₹71.74/litre and diesel at ₹59.40/litre. Delhi is the least expensive among Mumbai, Bengaluru, Chennai, Kolkata and Delhi.
