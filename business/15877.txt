Brent crude fell $2 to $58.53 a barrel on Thursday, after hitting a two-month high of $63 on Tuesday as U.S. crude stocks rose by 14.3 million barrels last week compared with analysts' expectations for an increase of 3.2 million barrels. Meanwhile, production from world's largest exporter Saudi Arabia may increase to 10 million barrels/day, according to consultancy PIRA.
