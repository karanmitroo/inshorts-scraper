The State bank of India on Monday became the first domestic bank to open a branch in Myanmar's capital Yangon, Chairperson Arundhati Bhattacharya said. This is SBI's 54th foreign branch and extends its presence in 37 countries through 198 offices, she added. The trade between the two nations has grown from ₹2,182 crore in 1997-98 to ₹13,652 crore in 2015-16.
