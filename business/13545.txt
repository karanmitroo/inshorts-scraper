The Nikkei India Services Purchasing Managers’ Index (PMI), an indicator of the services industry’s economic health, reportedly hit a five-month low of 50.1 in November, from an eight-month high of 53.2 in October. The business expectations sub-index fell below 50 to 49.9 for the first time in the survey’s 10-year history. An above-50 reading indicates growth while below-50 signals decline.
