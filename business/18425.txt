India's foreign exchange reserves dived by a hefty $2.995-billion to $277.167-billion, the RBI on Friday said.  The total reserves had increased $960.2-million to $280.162-billion last week. Foreign currency assets include the effect of appreciation or depreciation of the non-US currencies, such as euro, pound and yen, held in the reserves. During the week, the gold reserves dropped $808.5-million at $20.747-billion.
