Thirty-seven banks, mainly public sector ones, posted a net 26.8% increase in non-performing assets (NPAs) or bad loans during the 12-month period ended September. In comparison, the rise was 16.9% in the year-ago period. Further, overall NPAs currently stand at ₹3.36 lakh crore, with the last 12 months ended September accounting for ₹71,000 crore, according to credit rating agency CARE.
