Annual spending of $61.11 billion (around ₹4.1 lakh crore), or 3.77% of the GDP, is required on various job generation programmes like the MGNREGA to tackle levels of extreme poverty, UN-body International Labour Organisation (ILO) said on Wednesday. Around 23 crore people live below the extreme poverty level in India, with a daily income of less than $1.90 (around ₹127).
