Amid the International Monetary Fund slashing the global growth forecast, continuous outflow by foreign investors, dismal results for December quarter and soft rupee, the benchmark indices remained flat in the week ended January 22. In the five trading sessions, the BSE Sensex plunged 19.38 points to 24,435.66, while NSE Nifty dipped 15.35 points to settle the week at 7,422.45.
