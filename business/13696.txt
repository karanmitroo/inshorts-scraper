Gold imports plunged to $1.7 billion in October, a 59.5% fall against $4.20-billion import last year. Meanwhile, oil imports also dipped by 45.3% to $6.8 billion year-on-year. Total imports shrank 15.1% to $232 billion between April-October, and exports contracted 17.6% to $154.29 billion in the same period, thereby narrowing the trade deficit of the current fiscal to $77.76 billion.
