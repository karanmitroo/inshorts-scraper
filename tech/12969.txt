Tizen, a new Linux-based open-source OS for smartphones, developed by a global collaboration of tech firms to rival Google's Android and Apple's iOS will be launched in few months and the first phones having Tizen will be sold from March-end, Japanese tele-operator NTT Docomo said Wednesday. The Tizen Association includes firms like Intel, Samsung, Fujitsu and LG, Huawei and Vodafone.
