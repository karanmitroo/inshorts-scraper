The PMO announced India's plans to launch the Chandrayaan-2, the 2nd lunar satellite mission, during the year 2017-18 on Thursday. The indigenous satellite will have an Orbiter, Lander and Rover configuration, which will be launched by Geosynchronous Satellite Launch Vehicle from Satish Dhawan Space Centre, Sriharikota. Chandrayaan-1 was launched into the moon's orbit successfully in October 2008.
