According to Fortune magazine's analysis, Apple's shares have grown 744% since first iPhone was launched on June 29, 2007. This means Apple shares bought for $100 (₹6,460 as per current exchange rate) a decade ago would be worth $844 (₹54,500) now, not including reinvested dividends. Meanwhile, S&P 500 companies have risen just 62% in the same period, Fortune added.
