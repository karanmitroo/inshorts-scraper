Nintendo's Hiroshi Yamauchi (85), a pioneer in video-game industry, died from Pneumonia on Thursday. He took over his family business at 22 and led the company for over five decades, transforming Nintendo into a global byword for video-games.  Nintendo's first gaming console 'Family computer' laid the foundations of modern video-game industry while its success skyrocketed with the legendary 'Super-Mario' series.
