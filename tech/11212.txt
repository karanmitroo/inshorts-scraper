On May 11, the IISc inaugurated India's most powerful supercomputer, SahasraT built at a cost of $13 million. With a peak performance of 1.46 petaflops (1 petaflop = 10^15 mathematical computations per second), SahasraT overtakes Aaditya (ranked 71 globally) at the Indian Institute of Tropical Meteorology. China houses the world's fastest supercomputer with a peak performance of 33.86 petaflops.
