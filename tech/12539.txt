Microsoft has announced Android-powered Nokia X2 - the first device launched by Nokia since its devices and services division was bought by Microsoft. The X2 sports a 4.3-inch touch display, while it is powered by a 1.2GHz dual-core Snapdragon processor. It is dual-SIM, includes 15GB of on-device storage and has a five-megapixel rear-facing camera. The device is priced at US$135.
