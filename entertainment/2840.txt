Filmmaker-actor Lekh Tandon, who gave Shah Rukh Khan his first break with the TV serial 'Dil Dariya', passed away on Sunday aged 88. He had been unwell and bedridden for the last few months. Tandon is known for directing 'Amrapali', which was India's entry to Oscars 1967, and for acting in films like 'Swades', 'Paheli' and 'Chennai Express'.
